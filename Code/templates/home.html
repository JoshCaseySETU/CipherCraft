Module 1: The Start of Cryptography

Topics:

  • Why it began? - Brief History of why cryptography was invented
  • Caesar Cipher
  • Vigenère Cipher
  • Fence Cipher

Breakdown:

The first module provides a brief history of cryptography and introduces foundational ciphers such as the Caesar, Vigenère, and Fence ciphers.

Module 2: Where it is now

Topics:

  • Symmetrical and Asymmetrical
  • Block Ciphers and Stream Ciphers
  • One-Time Pad
  • Methods of Key Exchange - Diffie-Hellman key exchange protocol

Breakdown:

Module two explores modern encryption methods including symmetrical and asymmetrical cryptography, block ciphers, stream ciphers, and key exchange protocols like Diffie-Hellman.

Module 3: Advanced Encryption Standard (AES)

Topics:

  • AES Process
  • Electronic Codebook (ECB) Mode
  • Cipher Block Chaining (CBC) Mode
  • Counter (CTR) Mode

Breakdown:

Module three delves into the Advanced Encryption Standard (AES), explaining its operation, different modes such as ECB, CBC, and CTR, and their respective advantages and disadvantages.

Module 4: Hashing Algorithms

Topics:

  • What is a Hashing Algorithm
  • Strength of hashing
  • Different types - SHA1, MD5, SHA256
  • When and where it’s used

Breakdown:

Module four focuses on hashing algorithms, their properties, different types such as SHA1, MD5, SHA256, and their applications in securing passwords, files, and maintaining data integrity.

Module 5: Key Management

Topics:

  • Importance of Key Management
  • Key Generation
  • Key Storage
  • Key Life cycle diagram

Breakdown:

The final module emphasizes the importance of key management, covering key generation, storage methods, and the life cycle of a key including rotation, revocation, and expiration.
